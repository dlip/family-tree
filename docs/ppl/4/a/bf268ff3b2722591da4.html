Event Note

Margaret Mary Ann Lipscombe, formerly Bickell, petitioned for a dissolution of her marriage with Richard Lipscombe, on the ground of desertion. Petitioner said she married the respondent, who was a bricklayer, in August, 1877, at Petersham, according to the rites of the Presbyterian Church. They lived in different parts of Sydney till February, 1897, and there were ten children of the marriage. Her husband told her he was going to Captain's Flat to carry out a contract, and he went away and did not again return. They were living at the time in West- street, North Sydney. She had given him no cause for leaving. A decree nisi was granted, to be moved absolute in three months.
